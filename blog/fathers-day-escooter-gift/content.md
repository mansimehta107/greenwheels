# Give Dad the Ride of His Life This Father's Day

*Forget the socks and aftershave. This year, give your father something he'll genuinely use every day — a powerful e-scooter built for Irish roads, Irish weather, and the daily Dublin grind.*

---

## Why Dad Deserves an E-Scooter

Let's be honest — dads are famously hard to buy for. But this Father's Day, there's a gift that ticks every box: practical, exciting, money-saving, and just a bit cool. An electric scooter isn't another gadget that'll gather dust in the shed. It's a daily tool that'll change how your father gets around.

Think about your dad's typical week. The morning commute through Dublin traffic, quick runs to Tesco for milk, picking up a takeaway on a Friday evening, or heading out for a spin along the canal on a sunny Saturday. An e-scooter handles all of it — quietly, cheaply, and without the headache of finding parking on Capel Street.

With petrol prices in Ireland hovering around €1.70 per litre, the average Dublin commuter spends well over €2,000 a year just getting to work and back. An e-scooter costs roughly 10 cent to fully charge. Over a year, your dad could save enough to take the whole family on a holiday. That's not just a gift — it's a genuine upgrade to his quality of life.

And let's not forget the fun factor. Every dad secretly wants a new piece of tech to show off to the neighbours. An e-scooter that hits 50km/h and has an 80km range? He'll be talking about it at the barbecue for months.

---

## How Dad Will Use It

- **The daily commute:** Skip the Luas queues and M50 tailbacks. Fold the scooter, hop on the DART for part of the journey, then ride the last few kilometres to the office in minutes.
- **Weekend spins with the kids:** Head down to the Phoenix Park or along the Royal Canal Greenway for a proper family adventure on two wheels.
- **Quick trips to the shops:** Nipping to Lidl or the local butcher doesn't require warming up the car. Grab the scooter, be there in five minutes, and skip the car park circus entirely.
- **Beating Dublin traffic:** Whether it's roadworks on the quays or another bus lane closure, Dad glides right past it all. No stress, no delays, no road rage.
- **Exploring new trails and routes:** With up to 80km of range, there's nothing stopping Dad from discovering parts of Dublin — or further afield — he's never seen before. Howth Head, Bray Promenade, the Dodder Greenway — all within easy reach.
- **The evening takeaway run:** Volunteering to collect the chipper on a Friday night becomes something Dad actually looks forward to.
