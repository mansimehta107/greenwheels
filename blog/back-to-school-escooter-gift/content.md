## Why an E-Scooter Is the Perfect Gift for Students

September rolls around and the same headaches return: packed buses, expensive Leap cards, fights over the family car, and mornings lost waiting at draughty bus stops. Whether your son or daughter is heading into secondary school, starting their first year at TCD, UCD, or DCU, or returning for another semester at UL, NUI Galway, or Maynooth, there's one gift that cuts through all of it — an electric scooter.

Think about how students actually get around. They dash between lectures scattered across sprawling campuses. They nip to the library at odd hours. They commute from digs in Rathmines to classes in the city centre, or from student housing in Castletroy to the UL campus. Short, repetitive trips that are too far to walk comfortably but hardly worth a bus fare. An e-scooter covers these journeys in minutes, folds up to slide under a desk or into a locker, and costs virtually nothing to run.

For parents, the maths speaks for itself. A monthly student Leap card runs around €50, meaning you'll spend over €400 across the academic year on buses alone — and that doesn't count the days they miss the connection and end up in a taxi. Our most affordable scooter starts at just €250 and charges for pennies. It pays for itself before Christmas.

Then there's the generation factor. Today's students care deeply about the planet. Giving them a zero-emission way to travel says you share their values. No exhaust fumes, no congestion, just clean, quiet scooting through cycle lanes and campus paths. It's a gift that's practical, thoughtful, and future-forward all at once.

At Green Wheels Scooters, we've kitted out students from colleges right across Ireland. Every scooter comes with a full warranty, free delivery nationwide, and the backing of our Dublin 7 service centre — so if anything ever needs attention, we're right here.

## How Students Will Use It

### From Campus to City — Every Day, Sorted

- **Campus commute:** Beat the morning rush from student accommodation to lectures. No waiting for the 46A or fighting for a bike-share — just unfold, ride, and lock up outside the lecture hall in minutes.
- **Library and study runs:** When the library seat you need is on the far side of campus, or you're hopping between the arts block and the science building, an e-scooter turns a 20-minute walk into a 5-minute glide.
- **Part-time job commute:** Plenty of students juggle evening or weekend shifts. An e-scooter gets them to work on time without depending on patchy late-night bus services — and saves every cent of that pay cheque for rent and books.
- **Socialising across town:** From house parties in Drumcondra to pub quizzes in Temple Bar, an e-scooter makes spontaneous plans possible without worrying about Nitelink timetables or surge-priced taxis.
- **Grocery and errand runs:** A quick scoot to Lidl, Aldi, or the local Centra beats hauling bags on a bus. Throw a backpack on, ride there in minutes, and get back before the kettle boils.
- **Last-mile from bus or DART:** Live a bit further out? Ride the DART or bus for the main stretch, then unfold the scooter for the last kilometre to campus or home. It's the ultimate multimodal combo that saves both time and money.
