### Spring Adventures & Paddy's Day Fun

- **Navigating parade day crowds:** Skip the gridlocked streets and packed buses on March 17th. An e-scooter lets you zip between viewing spots, dodge the crowds, and actually enjoy the parade instead of spending half the day trying to get into town.
- **Spring rides through Dublin:** As the days get longer and the weather turns, Dublin transforms. Cruise along the Grand Canal, cut through Merrion Square, or ride down to Sandymount Strand on those first golden evenings of the year.
- **Visiting friends for celebrations:** Paddy's Day is for gathering — house parties, pub sessions, family dinners. An e-scooter gets you between them all without worrying about parking, taxi surges, or who's driving.
- **Avoiding traffic on the bank holiday:** The St. Patrick's Day long weekend turns every main road into a car park. While drivers are white-knuckling the N11, you'll be breezing past on two wheels with the smuggest look in Ireland.
- **Exploring the Wild Atlantic Way:** If you're heading west for the weekend, throw the scooter in the boot and ride sections of the Wild Atlantic Way that most tourists never see. Clifden, Doolin, Dingle — all better on a scooter.
- **Phoenix Park and beyond:** With over 700 hectares to explore, Phoenix Park is practically made for e-scooters. Ride past the Áras, wave at the deer, stop for coffee at the Farmleigh estate, and still have battery left for a lap of the Fifteen Acres.
